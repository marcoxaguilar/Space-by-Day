How Much Does a Kitchen Remodel Really Cost in Chicago?

A 2026 pricing guide for homeowners in Lemont, IL and the Chicago suburbs — with real cost tiers, budget breakdowns, and one strategy that can save you thousands before construction starts.

If you're a homeowner in the Chicago metro area planning a kitchen remodel, the first question is always the same: what's this actually going to cost?

The answer depends on your kitchen size, material choices, whether you're changing the layout, and where you live. But one thing is consistent — Chicago kitchen remodels cost more than national averages. Stricter building codes, competitive skilled labor, and the realities of working in older homes all push prices higher than what most online calculators suggest.

Here's what Chicago-area homeowners are actually paying in 2026, broken down by tier so you can plan realistically.

Where Does the Money Go?

Understanding how your kitchen remodel budget breaks down helps you make smarter decisions about where to invest — and where to save.

Cabinetry: 35–40% of Total Budget

Cabinets are consistently the largest single expense. On a $60,000 kitchen remodel, expect to spend roughly $21,000–$24,000 on cabinetry alone. Semi-custom options with soft-close hardware and pull-out shelving deliver the best balance of quality and value for most homeowners.

Labor & Installation: 25–30%

Skilled tradespeople — plumbers, electricians, tile installers, carpenters — command competitive rates in the Chicago area. Labor costs tend to run higher in the city and inner suburbs due to permitting complexity, parking logistics, and the challenges of working in older buildings.

Countertops: 10–15%

Quartz remains the most popular countertop choice for mid-range Chicago kitchen remodels, running $50–$150 per square foot installed. Butcher block is an increasingly popular option for island surfaces, offering warmth and character at a lower price point.

Appliances: 8–12%

A quality mid-range appliance package typically falls between $5,000 and $12,000. Professional-grade appliances can push that to $20,000–$30,000+ for serious home cooks.

Everything Else: 15–20%

Flooring, backsplash, lighting, plumbing fixtures, paint, permits, and design fees make up the remaining balance. Chicago building permits alone can run $1,800–$4,200 depending on scope.

Why Chicago Kitchen Remodels Cost More

If you've been comparing your quotes to national averages and wondering why they're higher, here's why Chicago is different:

  • Strict building codes — Most significant kitchen renovations in Chicago require permits, inspections, and code-compliant electrical and plumbing work
  • Aging housing stock — Older homes often have outdated wiring, galvanized pipes, or structural quirks that add unexpected costs once walls are opened
  • Competitive labor market — Skilled contractors in the Chicago area are in high demand, and quality tradespeople charge accordingly
  • Delivery and access — Suburban homes are easier to work in, but city and condo projects face elevator restrictions, limited working hours, and delivery surcharges

Pro tip: The most expensive kitchen remodel mistake isn't choosing the wrong countertop — it's approving a design you can't clearly picture, then making changes during construction. Change orders during a Chicago kitchen remodel can add 15–25% to your total cost.
